About Me

As an international student proudly born and raised in Indonesia, I have traveled far away across the world to be here in the United States. What brought me over to this strange and foreign land were many reasons, including a very important one: the desire to learn. As we know it, the United States is home to many of the world’s greatest tech giants that revolutionized the way human beings interact with their surroundings. If given the opportunity, who would not wish to travel and learn from the world's most innovative minds?

With that being said, I am willing to go the extra mile to make myself stand out. It is simply a product of my motivation for not letting great opportunities go to waste. To do this, I live by a principle that my parents have always enlightened me with:

"In life, we never stop learning."

I am constantly working towards developing and expanding my skillsets both in and outside of my academic coursework, while having a great time exploring new things.

In this webpage, you will find some of my proudest projects to date. But with this keep-learning mindset that I adhere to, there will be more to come...

Projects

Caraoke

Python, C++, Spotify API, Arduino IDE, Arduino Genuino 101

Caraoke is a lyrics display machine that is designed specifically to fit right into the air vents of a vehicle. As you go on road trips with friends and family, singing along to a tune may seem a bit dry if you're not too familiar with the song. So, for the Bitcamp 2022 Hackathon, my two other teammates and I created a versatile karaoke machine that displays the lyrics to any song currently playing on your Spotify account.

It was our first time working with Arduinos, so it was a great learning experience for all of us.

Bank Application

Java, JavaFX, FXML, Abstract Data Structures

BankApplication helps bank managers organize every inch of detail: from bank branches in various locations to individual customer account data. It was my first personal project outside of my academic coursework intended to enforce my understanding of abstract data structures.

It was also my first time working with JavaFX using FXMl as I transitioned from using Swing. I was mostly inspired on how software applications could make life so much easier, just like how this application may facilitate bank managers.

Magniflyer (WIP)

Java, JavaFX, FXML, Graph ADT

Magniflyer is an interactive application to help users search for cheap available flights across the United States. To simulate an airline route, a Graph data structure is implemented to locate paths through various destinations. This project was also an attempt to incorporate better designs using JavaFX.

UNIX Filesystem

C, UNIX

This project allows users to simulate a generic filesystem using some of the most basic UNIX commands. The whole program was written in ANSI C, and is heavily dependent on the concept of memory allocation since the language does not comprise of a garbage collection system.
